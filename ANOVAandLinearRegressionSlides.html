Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) provides a method for comparing group means across three or more groups

The hypotheses for an ANOVA test are:

\[\begin{array}{lcl} H_0 & : & \mu_1 = \mu_2 = \cdots = \mu_k\\ H_a & : & \text{At least one of the group means is different}\end{array}\]

Intuition for ANOVA

The main ideas behind ANOVA are somewhat simple…

  1. Ignore the groups all together and calculate the variability from the overall mean

  2. Calculate the variability from the group means

  3. Calculate how much of the total variability is explained by differences between the group means (\(\text{SS}_{\text{Total}} = \text{SS}_{\text{Between}} + \text{SS}_{\text{Within}}\))

  4. Standardize the variability measures by turning them into averages

  5. Compare the average between group variability to the average within group variability

    • If the between and within group variability are similar, then there is no evidence that group means differ
    • If the between and within group variability differ, then that is evidence that the group means differ from one another

Mechanics of ANOVA

Let’s talk a bit about how ANOVA will work

  1. Calculate the overall mean (\(\bar{x}\)) of all the observed data, ignoring the groups.
  2. Compute the total sum of squares (\(\text{SS}_{\text{Total}}\)) to measure the overall variability in the data: \(\text{SS}_\text{Total} = \sum (x - \bar{x})^2\)
  3. For each group, calculate the mean (\(\bar{x}_i\)) and the within-group sum of squares \(\left(\text{SS}_{\text{Within}}\right)\), which measures the variability within each group: \(\displaystyle{\text{SS}_\text{Within} = \sum_{\left(\text{groups, } i\right)} \sum_{\left(\text{observations, } x\right)} (x - \bar{x}_i)^2}\)
  4. Subtract the within-group variability from the total variability to get the between-group sum of squares (\(\text{SS}_{\text{Between}}\)), which measures how much the group means differ: \(\text{SS}_\text{Between} = \text{SS}_\text{Total} - \text{SS}_\text{Within}\)
  5. Compute the mean squares:
    • Mean Square Between (\(\text{MS}_{\text{Between}} = \text{SS}_{\text{Between}} / \left(\text{num_groups} - 1\right)\))
    • Mean Square Within (\(\text{MS}_{\text{Within}} = \text{SS}_{\text{Within}} / \left(\text{num_observations} - \text{num_groups}\right)\))
  6. The F-statistic is the ratio of these mean squares: \(\displaystyle{F = \frac{\text{MS}_\text{Between}}{\text{MS}_\text{Within}}}\)
